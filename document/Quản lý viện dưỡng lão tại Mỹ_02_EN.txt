To build a comprehensive nursing home management software (commonly referred to as an EHR/EMR & ERP system for Long-Term Care), the platform must be divided into interconnected core modules that seamlessly share data.
Below is the architectural breakdown of these core modules, categorized into 4 primary functional groups:
I. Clinical & Resident Care Group
This is the "heart" of the system, where nurses, physicians, and certified nursing assistants (CNAs) interact on a daily basis.
1. Intake & Electronic Health Records (EHR) Module
Digital Charting: Stores comprehensive demographics, medical history, allergy lists, emergency contacts, and legal guardianships (such as Power of Attorney - POA).
Intake Assessments: A suite of digital tools to evaluate vital signs and Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)—such as bathing, eating, transferring, and toileting—to automatically calculate and classify the resident's Level of Care (LOC).
2. Care Planning Module
Goal Setting: Allows the Director of Nursing (DON) to establish individualized, Person-Centered Care Plans on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis.
Automated Task Assignment: Translates clinical care plans into actionable daily task checklists for CNAs, accessible via tablets or mobile devices.
3. Electronic Medication Administration Record (eMAR) Module
Smart Med-Pass Scheduling: Displays a real-time list of residents requiring medication within specific time windows, utilizing color-coded alerts (e.g., Green: Administered, Yellow: Upcoming, Red: Overdue).
Advanced Verification: Supports barcode scanning of the resident’s wristband and the medication packaging to strictly enforce the "5 Rights" of medication administration (Right resident, Right medication, Right dose, Right route, Right time).
Pharmacy Integration: Automatically transmits refill requests to partner long-term care (LTC) pharmacies when a resident's medication inventory hits a predefined reorder threshold.
II. Operations & Workforce Group
4. Staff Scheduling Module
Mandated Staffing Ratios: The system automatically calculates the required number of nurses and CNAs based on the current resident census and acuity levels to ensure strict regulatory compliance.
Shift Bourse (Open Shift Management): Enables staff to view and pick up open shifts, or swap shifts with peers via a mobile app, requiring only a one-tap approval from management.
5. Dining & Dietary Module
Dietary Filter Integration: Manages daily menus in alignment with physician orders (e.g., diabetic-friendly, mechanical soft, pureed, or low-sodium diets).
Point of Sale / Tableside Ordering: Staff use tablets to take meal orders in the dining room, and the system automatically blocks any food items containing ingredients that trigger a resident's documented allergies.
III. Billing & Financials Group
6. Billing & Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) Module
Multi-Payer Billing: Automatically splits claims and invoices (e.g., generating an invoice where insurance covers 80% and the family/private pay covers the remaining 20%).
Claims Management: Integrates standard HIPAA EDI 837 transaction codes to submit claims to public (Medicare/Medicaid) or private commercial insurance clearinghouses, and ingests EDI 835 files to streamline Denial Management.
IV. Compliance & Engagement Group
7. Incident & Risk Management Module
Incident Reporting: Digitalized workflows to immediately document falls, injuries, or medication errors. The system automatically locks the affected resident's chart at the time of the incident to preserve records for state surveyors.
Wound Management: Allows clinicians to securely capture photos of pressure ulcers (bedsores), log measurements, and track healing progress over time on HIPAA-compliant storage for remote physician review.
8. Family Portal Module
Operational Transparency: A dedicated mobile application for residents' family members to view real-time health updates, scheduled recreational activities, daily menus, and securely pay care invoices online.
💡 System Design Note
When developing these modules, the system architecture must strictly adhere to the principle of Centralized Data Integration.
Example of Data Flow: When Module 1 (EHR) updates a resident's status indicating a decline in mobility $\rightarrow$ Module 2 (Care Plan) automatically injects a "Transfer Assistance" task for the CNAs $\rightarrow$ Module 4 (Scheduling) recalibrates floor staffing to account for higher acuity $\rightarrow$ Module 6 (Billing) dynamically updates the monthly invoice to reflect the new, elevated Level of Care (LOC) rate.
